About Me

If you asked my family and friends to describe me in a couple of words, they would say a sports fanatic and Indian TV binge watcher. And, they're right! Most of what I do really falls into these two categories. I have had a passion for sports since I was 9 years old and have recently developed an interest in sports analytics. I started watching sports podcasts on Youtube, and after a few weeks, this became a daily routine. So, if you are looking for someone to have a sports conversation with, well look no further I got you covered! Similarly, I have a passion for Indian shows and movies, and I watch them on different apps including Tentkotta, HotStar and Einthusan.

Knowing that my interest was in Data Analytics, I wanted to do a major in college that would guide me in this direction. For this reason, I selected Computer Science as my first choice major and am currently doing a Masters in Computer Science with an emphasis in Statistics at Texas A&M University.

Professional Image

My first taste of programming came in my sophomore year of high school when I learned Python in my introductory Computer Science class. I liked the language because it was written in a syntax that could be read like a sentence. The following year, I took AP Computer Science, and became exposed to Java, the first language I learned where you had to explicitly declare the data type when making a variable.

I did an Informational Technology internship this past summer at Tokio Marine HCC, an insurance company, and I worked with another intern on a Security Automation project. The Security Automation project helped me gain exposure to Microsoft's automation tool, called Microsoft Flow. I had never heard of this tool before, but after completing the internship, I am now able to create workflows that can automate manual processes. Currently, I am doing research with Dr. Shipman in the CSCE department in the area of Prediction Games. Take a look at my resume.
